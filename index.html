KTH Royal Institute of Technology student repositories

Since its founding in 1827, KTH Royal Institute of Technology in Stockholm has grown to become one of Europe’s leading technical and engineering universities, as well as a key centre of intellectual talent and innovation. We are Sweden’s largest technical research and learning institution and home to students, researchers and faculty from around the world dedicated to advancing knowledge.

Who can contribute to github.com/KTHStudents?

Active students and teachers in KTH are the primary contributors in the github.com/KTHStudents organisation.

 

What will github.com/KTHStudents be used for?

The github.com/KTHStudents organisation primary use is for students/teachers to publish and collaborate in different software projects during their education in KTH.

Can i expect to keep my account on github.com/KTHStudents after my education?

Accounts will be disabled and students/teachers will be not be able to access projects after that time period.

 

Adding new repositories

All students and teachers at KTH can publish software or eqvivalent on github.com/KTHStudents.

 

Can staff/faculty use github.com/KTHStudents?

Staff/Faculty are not allowed to use the github.com/KTHSTudents organisation for their projects.

Which github should staff/faculty use?

Staff/Faculty can use the following github systems/services for their work.

KTH Github - The on premises solution for git repositories
Github.com/KTH - The KTH public cloud solution for staff/faculty

 

Published code

Each student/teacher is responsible for the code published on github.com/KTHStudents.

KTH's default license for open source distributed by KTH is the MIT license.
It's up to each user to decide the licensing for their project.

 

Contact us

Github.com/KTHStudents is maintained by the IT-department at KTH. Contact us on it-support@kth.se.
